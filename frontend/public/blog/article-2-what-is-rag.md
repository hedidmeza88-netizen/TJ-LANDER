# What is RAG? (And Why It’s Better Than Fine-Tuning)

In the rush to adopt AI, many executives think they need to "train" a model on their data. They imagine feeding thousands of PDFs into a black box to teach it about their business.

In the AI world, we call this **Fine-Tuning**. And for 90% of business use cases, it is the **wrong approach**.

It is expensive, slow, and hard to update. If you fine-tune a model on your sales data today, it will be outdated tomorrow.

## Enter RAG: The Open-Book Test

The better alternative is **Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG)**.

Think of it like a student taking a test:
*   **Fine-Tuning** is like forcing the student to *memorize* the entire textbook. If the textbook changes, they have to study all over again.
*   **RAG** is like letting the student take an *open-book test*. When you ask a question, the student looks up the relevant page, reads the answer, and summarizes it for you.

## Why RAG Wins for Enterprise

### 1. Accuracy & Citations
Because RAG "looks up" the answer in your actual documents, it ensures accuracy. At Axion Minds, our systems provide **citations** with every answer.
> *Answer: "The project deadline is Q3." (Source: Project_Alpha_Kickoff.pdf, page 12)*

### 2. Real-Time Knowledge
With RAG, there is no training time. As soon as you upload a new policy document or sales report to your database, the AI can answer questions about it **instantly**.

### 3. Lower Cost
Training a model requires massive GPU power. RAG uses efficient Vector Search, which costs a fraction of the price and runs on standard cloud infrastructure.
